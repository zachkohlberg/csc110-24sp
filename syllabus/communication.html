Communication and Office Hours

Office Hours

My normal office hours are Tuesday from 2:00-3:30 PM EST in TH130. Feel free to stop by during that time if you want to talk or work on assignments.

I am an adjunct (part-time) faculty and do not have an office on campus. I will not usually be around outside of class meetings and office hours, but I don't mind scheduling a remote meeting if you need to talk but can't make it to office hours that week.

Blackboard

You should be able to find all course material and links to submit each assignment on this class's Blackboard page. If material is hosted on another site, then there should still be a link on Blackboard. Remember that labs and lectures are separate courses with separate Blackboard pages.

Email

My email is zckohlberg@qu.edu. I usually check my email daily during the week and will try to respond to all emails within 48 hours. If you email me after the morning on Friday or over the weekend, then I may not get back to you until Monday. You're still welcome to email me at any time during the semester (or after, I love to hear how my former students are doing).

I can answer most questions via email if you don't want to ask in person. If you're asking for help with an assignment, coding issue, or software, then please try to include as much information as possible and be specific about what you want to know (this blog post has excellent advice for asking productive questions). The more relevant information you include in your email the faster I'll be able to help you.

If you need me to do something more involved than answer some questions, such as review your code or help you debug a program, then you should meet with me during office hours. I'm happy to help you with coding, debugging, and other tasks as long as you're the one doing the task. You should not come to office hours or email me expecting me to do your work for you.

Slack/Discord

I may set up a Slack workspace or Discord server for the class. If I do, then it will be linked on Blackboard. It gives us another channel for communication, and it tends to be quicker and less formal than email. If we do have a Slack or Discord, then its use will be optional.
