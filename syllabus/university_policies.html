University Policies

This section covers some university policies and resources that you should be aware of. I have additional information about how these policies pertain to our class, but I'm going to keep that separate.

QU Academic Catolog

The QU Academic Catalog includes most of the resources and policies that I'll be linking in this section: https://catalog.qu.edu/. This includes the undergrad student handbook, the academic integrity policy, and the disability policy.

Learning Commons

I highly recommend making use of the Learning Commons any time you need help with work for this class. There should be several peer tutors available for CSC and SER courses. Below is some additional information about the Learning Commons and their contact information.

The Learning Commons is a place for students to go when they want to achieve a level of performance they can’t reach on their own. In a setting of practice and growth, student resources are provided through Peer Educator programs, the Office of Student Accessibility, and Academic Development & Outreach professional staff. The Learning Commons can be found at the Mount Carmel Campus in the North wing of the Arnold Bernhard Library; and at the North Haven Campus on the third floor of the Law School (SLE-340). Students are encouraged to visit The Learning Commons for support with class content, to improve study skills, to consult on academic success strategies, and for general developmental advising needs.

Phone: 203-582-8628

Email: LearningCommons@Qinnipiac.edu

Website: https://www.qu.edu/student-life/support-services/

Academic Integrity

Here is a link to Quinnipiac University's Academic Integrity (AI) policy: https://catalog.qu.edu/university-policies/academic-integrity-policy/. This policy applies to all courses at QU. I will address Academic Integrity in detail later in the syllabus.

Accommodations

QU's policy on disabilities: https://catalog.qu.edu/university-policies/disability-policy/.

This is a standard statement about accommodations and disabilities that the university recommends including in all syllabi:

Quinnipiac University is committed to creating a learning environment that meets the needs of its diverse student body. If you anticipate or experience any barriers to learning in this course, please feel welcome to discuss your concerns with me.

If you have a disability, or think you may have a disability, you may also want to meet with the Office of Student Accessibility, to begin this conversation or to request reasonable accommodations. Quinnipiac University complies with the Americans with Disabilities Act and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973.

Please contact the Office of Student Accessibility by emailing access@qu.edu, or by calling 203-582-7600. If you have already been approved for accommodations through the Office of Student Accessibility, please meet with me so we can develop an implementation plan together.

Additionally, I want to say that your mental and physical health are more important than your classes. If you're struggling with class due to mental health issues or notice that you're developing unhealthy habits to cope with your academic workload, then please talk to someone as early as possible. I'll always try to work with you if you tell me about a problem you're dealing with, and the sooner we talk the more I can do to help you.
